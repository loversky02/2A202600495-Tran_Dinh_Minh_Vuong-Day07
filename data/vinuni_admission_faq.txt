VinUni Admission - Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

=== GENERAL QUESTIONS ===

Q: What is the application fee?
A: Under normal circumstances, applicants to VinUni who pass the application screening stage and are invited to the interview round are required to pay an application fee of 2,000,000 VND. However, applicants who submit during the Early Round will receive a waiver of this fee if they participate in VinUni Open Day or other admission events.

Q: Can I apply to multiple majors?
A: Yes, you can indicate your preferred majors in your application. The Admissions Council will consider your profile and recommend the most suitable program for you.

Q: What are the AACC Standards?
A: AACC stands for Aspiration, Ability, Academic, and Creativity. These are the core evaluation criteria VinUni uses to assess candidates beyond just grades and test scores.

Q: Do I need SAT or ACT scores to apply?
A: No, SAT or ACT scores are not required. However, high SAT and ACT scores will be an advantage for the university to consider, especially for scholarship applications.

=== ENGLISH PROFICIENCY ===

Q: What if I don't meet the English proficiency requirement?
A: If you do not meet the English proficiency requirement of IELTS 6.5 (or equivalent), you can take the English Placement Test to be placed in additional English proficiency classes through the Pathway English Program (usually taught in the summer semester).

Q: Which English tests does VinUni accept?
A: VinUni accepts:
- IELTS: 6.5 or higher, with no skill below 6.0
- TOEFL iBT: Minimum 79 with at least 20 in writing, 18 in speaking, 15 in reading, and 15 in listening
- Pearson Test of English (Academic): Minimum total score of 58 with no communication skill below 50
- Cambridge Advanced English (CAE): Minimum 176 with no skill below 169 in any part

Q: How long is the English proficiency certificate valid?
A: English proficiency test results are typically valid for 2 years from the test date. Please check with the Admissions Office for specific requirements.

=== SCHOLARSHIPS AND FINANCIAL AID ===

Q: Can I apply for both scholarships and financial aid?
A: Yes, candidates can apply for more than one type of scholarship/financial aid, but the maximum grant amount must not exceed 100% of the tuition fee.

Q: Are scholarships only for the first year?
A: No! VinUni offers scholarships throughout the entire four years of study, not limited to the first year only. Entry scholarships awarded to students upon enrollment will apply for the entire duration of their studies if you meet the retention criteria.

Q: What happens if my GPA drops below the scholarship requirement?
A: If you fail to meet the scholarship retention criteria, the scholarship percentage will be reduced by one scholarship tier (10%). For example, from full scholarship to 90%, or from 100% to 90%. However, if you improve your academic performance in the following academic year, you will regain the previously awarded scholarship level.

Q: How many students lose their scholarships?
A: The percentage of students who have their scholarships reduced is only about 2%. The majority of students at VinUni are able to maintain their merit scholarships and demonstrate good performance.

Q: Can scholarships be upgraded during my studies?
A: Entry scholarships awarded upon enrollment will not be upgraded. However, students will have the opportunity to participate in other scholarship/financial support sources during their studies such as the President's Scholarship, Provost's Scholarship, Dean's Scholarship, Change Maker awards, or scholarship programs offered by businesses and philanthropists.

Q: What is the difference between scholarships and financial aid?
A: Scholarships are merit-based, awarded for outstanding academic achievements and personal qualities. Financial aid is need-based, available for students who meet admission criteria but face financial difficulties. Both can be combined up to 100% of tuition fees.

Q: What is the Full Ride Scholarship?
A: The Full Ride Scholarship (President's Excellence Scholarship) covers 100% of tuition fees plus VND 34,965,000/year for living expenses such as accommodation and meals.

=== TUITION AND PAYMENT ===

Q: How much is the tuition fee at VinUni?
A: Listed tuition for undergraduate programs:
- All majors: ~815,850,000 VND (~35,000 USD) per year
- Nursing: 349,650,000 VND (approximately 15,000 USD) per year

However, all admitted candidates receive a 35% Fund for Education Development, so the actual tuition is:
- Nursing program: approximately 227 million VND per year
- Other programs: approximately 530 million VND per year

Q: When do I need to pay tuition?
A: Tuition fee payment will be made twice a year at the beginning of each main semester (Fall Semester and Spring Semester).

Q: What payment methods are available?
A: There are 2 payment methods:
1. Direct Visa card payment at the Accounting and Finance Department of VinUni
2. Online bank transfer through the Salesforce portal with detailed instructions sent via email

Q: What does the tuition fee include?
A: Tuition fees include training-practice fees, facilities, technology, student activities (orientation, medical services and school psychology counseling, academic advisors, career counselors, student council, clubs, sporting-cultural events, international exchange, business experiences).

=== INTERNATIONAL EXCHANGE ===

Q: Do I have to pay extra for international exchange programs?
A: No! VinUni students admitted to the peer-to-peer international exchange program will study at one of VinUni's partner universities for one or two semesters. Students are NOT required to pay tuition fee to the partner university and they will receive credit after participating in the exchange program. Tuition fee paid to VinUni remains the same.

Q: Which universities can I exchange to?
A: The majority of VinUni's partner universities are either in the Top 100 Universities globally or the world's leading university in related majors/fields. Partners include Cornell University, University of Pennsylvania, and many others. Details can be found at https://vinuni.edu.vn/global-exchange/

=== APPLICATION TIMELINE ===

Q: When should I apply?
A: There are three admission rounds:
1. Early Round: October 15, 2024 – January 15, 2025 (Application fee waived if you attend VinUni events)
2. Regular Round: February 15, 2025 – May 15, 2025
3. Rolling Round: June 15, 2025 – August 15, 2025

We recommend applying in the Early Round to maximize your chances and receive the application fee waiver.

Q: How long does the admission process take?
A: The timeline varies depending on when you submit your application and which round you apply in. Generally, you can expect to hear back within 4-6 weeks after submitting your complete application.

=== SPECIAL PROGRAMS ===

Q: What is the Pathway English Program?
A: The Pathway English Program is designed for students who do not meet the English proficiency requirement of IELTS 6.5 (or equivalent). Students take the English Placement Test and are placed in additional English proficiency classes, usually taught in the summer semester. The cost for this program is approximately 50,000,000 VND (around 2,000 USD), paid by the student.

Q: Are there special scholarships for specific groups?
A: Yes! VinUni offers several special scholarship programs that can be combined with merit-based scholarships:
- Special Academic Scholarship: 5% tuition for Nursing, Mechanical Engineering, and Electrical Engineering programs
- WIT (Woman in Tech) Scholarship: 5% tuition for female candidates pursuing careers in science and technology
- Vinschool-VinUni Articulation Scholarship: 5% tuition for Vinschool students
- Vingroup Family Scholarship: For children of Vingroup employees and leaders

=== STUDENT LIFE ===

Q: What makes VinUni different from other universities?
A: VinUni offers:
- World-class education with partnerships with top global institutions (Cornell, UPenn)
- Generous scholarship and financial aid programs (over VND 882 billion awarded)
- International exchange opportunities with Top 100 universities globally
- Modern facilities and strong industry connections
- A supportive environment where students can pursue both academic excellence and personal passions
- Small class sizes and personalized attention
- 100% English-taught curriculum with international faculty

Q: Can I pursue extracurricular activities while studying?
A: Absolutely! VinUni encourages students to develop holistically. Many students successfully balance rigorous academics with leadership roles in clubs, sports, arts, and entrepreneurship. Student success stories include students who combine medicine with dance, develop HR skills through club leadership, and pursue various passions alongside their studies.
